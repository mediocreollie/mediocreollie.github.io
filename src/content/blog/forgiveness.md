“We are forgetting how to forgive”

I grew up in a school that taught Christian values. It wasn’t a Christian school, but ethics and forgiveness were still treated as important things to teach children.

As I got older, that slowly faded. By secondary school we learned more about politics, public opinion and social identity, and around the same time social media started shaping how people viewed mistakes.

I remember seeing celebrities or public figures do something wrong and instead of people wanting growth or accountability, it often felt like people wanted permanent punishment. One mistake became their entire identity. The internet freezes people at their worst moment and keeps replaying it forever.

But this is not really about celebrities.

It is about normal people too. Friends you fell out with years ago. Family members you quietly resent. Old versions of yourself you still mentally punish even though you were younger, less experienced and trying to figure life out.

A lot of people carry around conversations they wish they could redo, apologies they never made or moments they handled badly. The strange thing is that the event is often long gone for everyone else, but we keep replaying it privately in our own heads.

The energy people spend reliving situations they cannot change is honestly exhausting. There are people I treated badly when I was younger that I wish I could apologise to, but sometimes apologising years later is more about easing your own guilt than helping the other person. Sometimes the kindest thing is simply learning from it and not repeating it.

Social media often pushes the idea that people cannot truly change, that mistakes define you permanently and forgiveness means weakness. I don’t really agree with that.

You do not have to forgive everyone. Some people genuinely hurt others in ways that should not be brushed aside. But I think we are becoming worse at letting go of smaller things, worse at allowing people space to grow and worse at forgiving ourselves for being imperfect.

I’m not especially religious now, but one thing I still respect from religious teaching is the idea that people are capable of redemption. Most humans are not static. They change constantly through experience, regret, maturity and time.

Only you can decide what deserves forgiveness. But I do think life becomes lighter when you stop dragging every old mistake behind you forever.

You don’t have to reopen every closed door. Sometimes forgiveness is just deciding not to carry the weight anymore.
