The job market has changed, I believe the era of relying on just your work experience to climb the career ladder is over in a lot of roles. 

We are in a time where the barrier to entrance is higher and lower than ever. You need to do much than your predecessors, is that fair? no, is it the reality we are in? yes. A lot of questions and roles even for entry level roles ask for examples of what you have built, managed or owned even at the entry level. 20 years ago this would have priced out most of the audience. It is now easier than ever to build something from scratch, whether that's a website, blog, youtube, social media, app or small business. You can do this for yourself, a local business, sports team or club. You can also learn how to do this easier than ever, if you can ask how to do something you can now find the answer easier than ever with how good LLM's have gotten recently. 

This has brought the floor of corperate jobs up, as people take on more and more responsibility they continue to have less spare time, preferring to hire existing experience rather than spare time for people with none. I believe this is going to lead to long term issues with a lack of qualified people further down the line.
