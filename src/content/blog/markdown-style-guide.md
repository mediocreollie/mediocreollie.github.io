The Trojan horse is a story that will continue to be told, how a seemingly innocent gift tricks a target into inviting a deceitful force into a secure space. This being particularly popularised in technology with Trojan horse viruses. But I have felt myself noticing these horses everywhere in the world in recent times, and these are only the ones I haven’t let get in.

A lot of media pulls on your emotion and empathy to try get something from you, whether that’s selling a small idea like a new perspective or knowledge on an issue, your time for ad revenue, to get you to purchase something, or political power. And the Trojan horse is a perfect way to hide your intentions and get people onboard.

There’s nothing wrong with flying the St George flag, but what if someone slipped a new meaning into it to make it ambiguous, causing a conflict between people who see it as its new meaning and people who use it for its original meaning, and then the someone who created the new meaning sweeps into debate those who believe in the original meaning. Because of course it’s ridiculous to attack someone for flying their nation’s flag.

But now that someone has built support with this group, what if they addressed other issues this group has and sells simple solutions as before, so when they start to offer solutions that blame a group they were logical before, so surely this is too?

Or what if it’s someone trying to help you out of a bad financial situation, or helping you look better, or convincing your beliefs, or supporting your mental health.

That’s what makes this modern version so effective, it hides that trust you have for people and releases the trick when your guard is dropped.

The reason I class these as a Trojan horse, not a sales funnel, is the intentional misleading of a group where the action you take at the end is so different to the action you thought you were taking at the beginning.

Whilst I have simplified a complex example, I believe it is important to identify these horses in our lives, as would you accept the horse from the Greeks knowing what you know now?
