      <h2>A quick version of my experience</h2>

      <p>I’ve worked across marketing, product, operations, commercial and AI
      adoption, mostly in roles where the job is to take something messy and
      make it clearer.</p>

      <p>That has meant building B2B marketing plans, improving customer
      experience reporting, supporting operational planning, helping people
      adopt AI tools, reviewing product ranges, clearing old stock, and working
      between teams who do not always speak the same language.</p>

      <p>A lot of how I work also comes from earlier experience: refereeing
      football matches, running a university boxing club, working in customer
      service, and learning how to deal with people properly.</p>

      <p>The common thread is taking practical problems and turning them into
      something easier to act on: a plan, a decision, a process, a project, a
      test, or just a clearer way forward.</p>

      <h2>What I bring</h2>

      <h3>Marketing that connects to the business</h3> <p>I’ve worked on B2B
      marketing, campaign planning, events, newsletters, website content and
      product marketing. I’m interested in marketing that does a job, not just
      activity for the sake of activity.</p>

      <h3>Commercial thinking without pretending to be a finance robot</h3> <p>I
      like understanding what is actually worth doing: what is selling, what is
      stuck, what needs fixing, and where time or money is being wasted.</p>

      <h3>Product and customer thinking</h3> <p>A lot of my work has sat around
      the same question: does this actually work for the person using it? That
      has applied to PPE ranges, airline customer journeys, internal tools and
      my own AI product builds.</p>

      <h3>AI adoption that is practical, not hype</h3> <p>I’ve worked on
      internal AI tools and adoption, including training, workshops, user
      feedback and helping people understand how tools fit into their day-to-day
      work.</p>

      <h3>Cross-functional delivery</h3> <p>Most of my experience has involved
      working between teams: marketing, sales, suppliers, developers,
      operations, customer teams, warehouse, airports, leadership and frontline
      users.</p>
